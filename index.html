Systemic Racism Is Real


Systemic racism looks at the way racism operates over all of society, not just in one-on-one interactions.


Systemic racism persists in our schools, offices, court system, police departments, and elsewhere. Why? When white people occupy most positions of decision-making power, people of color have a difficult time getting a fair shake, let alone getting ahead.


Wealth


According to one study, white families hold 90% of the national wealth, whereas black families hold 2.6%. For every $100 white families earn in income, black families earn just $57.30.


Employment


The unemployment rate for black Americans have been consistently twice that of whites African-Americans are 2x as likely to be unemployed. According to the data, black people with college degrees are twice as likely to be unemployed as all other graduates.


Criminal Justice


Although black people make up 13% of the population, they represent 40% of the prison population. Because if a black person and a white person each commit a crime, the black person has a better chance of being arrested.
